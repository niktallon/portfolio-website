üèó PROJECT

Project Overview

To allow people with specific nutritional needs to find and prepare recipes that are relevant to their lifestyle.

Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

The objective for this MVP is to gain validation from target users that such an app will be used to prepare quick meal that meets their nutrition goals, and find it very easy to follow the recipes

Hypotheses

We believe that providing a large variety of quick recipes for users who have a hard schedule and little time to cook, will result in users finding something suitable for their needs.

Success Metrics

  • Primary Success Metric: % of returning users
Supporting metrics:
  • % Increase in total weekly web visits
  • Usability of the filter tools
  • Style guide - Logo, color, typography
  • Reviews and feedback on recipes within the app

Research Method‚Ä®

Research Objective

Learn where a recipe platform geared towards weekly meal planning and nutrition could meet a need.

Questions

  • When do you use recipes?
  • How do you use websites or apps to help you with recipes?
  • What are your favorite features of any existing apps you use to help with recipes? If you don't use apps, why not and would there be any features that would make you start?
  • What don‚Äôt you like about any tools you currently use? Why?
  • What role does nutrition play in selecting recipes?
  • Do you use recipes more for yourself or group meals? or both?
  • To what percent do you feel you adhere to the recipe instructions?

User Research Results

Patterns that emerged

  • Performing a Google search for a specific dish is the go-to action
  • All participants do not invest much time cooking, especially due to the lack of time.
  • Allrecipes, pinterest and bon appetite came up a lot
  • Each person used recipes at a different step in the process, a clear pattern did not emerge
  • Google and social media covered a lot of needs vs a specific website or app
